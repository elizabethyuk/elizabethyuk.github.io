Research Question #1

Diversity and Inclusion in Music Education

Presentation Handout

Although music is offered in most public schools, it does not always ensure equitable access, participation, or representation. There has been growing attention on the need for greater diversity, equity, and inclusion within school music programs (Bond, 2017; Rector, 2025). Current research highlights persistent gaps in representation, with music performance settings remaining disproportionately White (Elpus & Abril, 2019). These demographics suggest that existing music teaching practices may not be effectively attracting or retaining students of color, indicating a need for a shift in pedagogical approach (Elpus, 2015). Music educators are challenged to move beyond traditional curricula and adopt more inclusive and culturally responsive practices that reflect the lived experiences of all students (Fleischman, 2024). Research shows that although schools are growing more racially and ethnically diverse, music education remains predominantly white. What does research reveal about effective strategies for creating a more inclusive and diverse music program?

Research Question #2

SEL and Ensemble Participation

Presentation Handout

What is the goal of music education? Is it solely to teach students about music, or are we also nurturing their growth as empathetic, responsible, and well-rounded individuals? Many music educators believe in the latter, recognizing the power of music to support students’ social and emotional development (Shaw, 2021; Váradi, 2022). This belief highlights the importance of integrating social-emotional learning (SEL) into the music classroom (Varner, 2019). While implementing SEL can be particularly challenging in large ensemble settings, these environments also offer unique opportunities for collaboration and the development of emotional well-being and interpersonal skills (Tahirbegi, 2023; Sutherland & Southcott, 2021). Understanding how to support students in this area is essential for creating meaningful, student-centered music experiences. What does research say about the relationship between SEL and experience of ensemble participation?

Research Question #3

Classroom Management in the Ensemble Setting

Presentation Handout

Today’s music educators are responsible for teaching large groups of students in ensemble settings such as band, orchestra, and choir—environments that differ significantly from general music classrooms. These settings present unique challenges, including maintaining student engagement across varying performance levels, managing classrooms without standard desks, protecting expensive instruments, and fostering collaboration and teamwork through music-making (Gordon, 2002; Byo & Sims, 2015). Understanding and implementing effective classroom management strategies tailored to the specific demands of the music ensemble is essential for creating a productive, safe, and meaningful learning environment (Langstaff, 2023; Martin, 2021). What does research reveal about classroom management strategies specific to large music ensembles?
